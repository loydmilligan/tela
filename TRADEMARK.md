The AGPL grants you broad rights to the *code* — to use, study, modify, self-host, and
redistribute it. It does **not** grant any right to the Marks. This is the standard
open-source split between code (licensed) and brand (reserved), and it's what prevents a
"rebrand and resell" of the project.

## What you may do

- Run, self-host, and modify tela for any purpose, under the AGPL.
- Fork the code and distribute your fork **under a different name and branding**.
- Refer to tela by name in truthful, descriptive ways — "compatible with tela",
  "a fork of tela", "based on tela" — as nominative fair use.

- Use the name "tela", the wordmark, or the logo as the name or branding of a
  redistributed or hosted version of the software (i.e. passing your distribution off
  as "tela").
- Use the Marks in a way that implies endorsement, affiliation, or that your service is
  the official tela.
- Use the Marks in a domain name, product name, or company name for a competing offering.

If you want to use the Marks beyond the above, or you want a commercial license to the
code without AGPL obligations, contact the maintainer.
